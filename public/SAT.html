SURYA Admission Test (SAT)

National Standardized Admission & Scholarship Examination

"Empowering Talent. Enabling Excellence. Building the Future."

View 2027 Syllabus Register Now

The SURYA ADMISSION TEST (SAT) is a prestigious, standardized, merit-based entrance examination designed to identify talented students for admission into Surya Educational Institutions and affiliated colleges. Inspired by the examination structure and transparency, SAT evaluates students' conceptual understanding, analytical thinking, problem-solving ability, and subject knowledge.

SAT serves as a single-window national-level examination for admissions, scholarships, and academic excellence recognition.

1. Vision

To establish India's most trusted and transparent admission examination that identifies deserving students based on merit, knowledge, creativity, and aptitude while promoting academic excellence across the nation.

2. Mission

  • Provide equal opportunities to all students.
  • Encourage conceptual learning instead of rote memorization.
  • Offer merit-based admissions.
  • Award scholarships to deserving candidates.
  • Build future leaders through quality education.

3. Objectives

  • Assess academic preparedness of students.
  • Evaluate logical reasoning and analytical skills.
  • Provide a fair and transparent admission process.
  • Reduce multiple entrance examinations.
  • Identify students eligible for scholarships.
  • Maintain national academic standards.

8. Question Types

  • Multiple Choice Questions (MCQ): Four options, one correct answer.
  • Multiple Select Questions (MSQ): Multiple correct answers.
  • Numerical Answer Type (NAT): No options, numerical value entry.
  • Case Study Questions: Real-life situations requiring analytical reasoning.

7. Examination Pattern

Part A — General Aptitude (15%)

English, Logical Reasoning, Quantitative Aptitude, Data Interpretation, Verbal Ability.

Marks: 30

Part B — Core Subject (70%)

Questions related to the selected discipline (e.g., Mathematics, Programming, Accountancy, Biology, etc.).

Marks: 140

Part C — Innovation & Critical Thinking (15%)

Evaluates Creativity, Problem Solving, Decision Making, Innovation, and Case Studies.

Marks: 30

11. Eligibility & 12. Age Limit

  • SAT-Junior: Completed Class 10
  • SAT-UG: Completed Class 12
  • SAT-PG: Bachelor's Degree
  • SAT-Professional: Graduation or relevant experience

Age Limit: No upper age limit. Candidates must satisfy course eligibility.

15. Result & 16. Validity

Results include: Raw Score, Normalized Score, Percentile, AIR, Category Rank, and Merit Position.

Score Validity: 3 Academic Years

20. Ranking System

The SURYA Admission Rank (SAR) uses Total Score, Subject Score, Aptitude Score, Innovation Score, and Tie-breaking Rules.

Ranks Issued: All India Rank (AIR), State Rank, Institution Rank.

21. Examination Integrity

  • AI-Based Remote Monitoring & Live CCTV
  • Biometric & Face Recognition
  • Aadhaar Verification (Optional)
  • Question Randomization
  • Secure Browser & End-to-End Encryption

22. Code of Conduct

Candidates must carry valid identification, reach the center early, avoid prohibited items, follow invigilator instructions, and maintain academic honesty.

Violations may result in disqualification.

23. Benefits of SAT

  • Single examination for multiple programs.
  • Merit-based admission with scholarship opportunities.
  • National-level recognition and standardized evaluation.
  • Transparent ranking system & online counseling.

24. Future Roadmap

  • AI-based adaptive testing & analytics.
  • International examination centers & collaborations.
  • Regional language support.
  • Digital scorecards & Blockchain-secured certificates.

25. Governing Authority

SURYA Examination Authority (SEA)

The SEA is an autonomous body responsible for conducting SAT, developing policies, ensuring fairness and transparency, publishing results, and managing counseling and admissions.
